# The starting point

> **Note for the team:** this is a placeholder MDX entry so the collection isn't empty. Replace the body with the real engagement once the client approves the case study.

The firm had been on Salesforce for six years. Three admins had come and gone. Two SIs had layered automations on top of each other. The result: every new opportunity required four duplicate-record warnings, three approval steps that no longer matched the org chart, and a quote document that pulled fields three of which had been deprecated two years earlier.

# What we did

- Ran a 2-week org health audit — security, automation, technical debt, reports.
- Built a prioritized backlog scored on effort and impact.
- Migrated 14 process builders and 9 workflow rules into 6 consolidated Flows.
- Retired 47 unused fields and 12 unused record types.
- Rebuilt the quote-to-cash flow with the head of revenue in the room.

# What changed

- Time-to-quote dropped from 28 minutes to 16.
- Average opportunity stage time reduced by 6 days.
- The team stopped "exporting to Excel to actually see the pipeline."

# What's next

We're now embedded as their admin-as-a-service partner: biweekly releases, quarterly roadmap, no full-time admin headcount needed.
